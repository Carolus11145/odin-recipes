Chakchouka

A Short Description

Chakchouka, or rather Shakshuka, is a Maghrebi and Israeli dish primarily made with egg and tomato. It is a simple dish often eaten for lunch or breakfast, but this is not an unbreakable rule.

These are the ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/3 cups chopped onion
  • 1 cup thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 2 1/2 cups chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 hot chili pepper (seeded and finely chopped)
  • 4 eggs

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Stir in the onion, bell peppers and garlic; cook and stir until the vegetables have softened and the onion has turned translucent. This should be about 5 minutes.
  2. Take a bowl and mix together the tomatoes, cumin, paprika, salt and chili pepper. Pour the mixture into the skillet to combine it with the vegetable mix.
  3. Simmer the mixture uncovered for around 10 minutes until the juices have cooked off.
  4. Create four openings in the mix and crack the eggs into them. Let the eggs cook for about 5 minutes (or a bit longer if you'd like the yolk slightly dryer).
  5. If you've followed the recipe correctly, you're dish should look like the image above (more or less). If so, well done! You may now dine on your delicious Chakchouka.
